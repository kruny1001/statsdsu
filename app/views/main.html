Capita Income in U.S.

We would like a simple but accurate measure of how the economic well-being of the typical American worker has been changing in recent years. Are the people we defined as middle class getting richer, poorer, or just running in place? A reasonable answer--though by no means the "right" answer--would be to calculate the change in per capita income in the United States over the course of a generation, which is roughly thirty years. Per capita incoe is a simple average:

Per capita income is a simple average: total income divided by the size of the population. By that measure, average income in the United States climbed from $7,787 in 1980 to $26,487 in 2010(the latest year for which the government has data)

Problem 1: To begin with the figures above are not adjusted for inflation.(A per capita income of $7,787 in 1980 is equal to about $19,600 when converted to 2010 dollars.) That's a relatively quick fix.

Problem 2: The bigger income is the average incoe in America is not equal to the income of the average American.
